## Part 3: The Kybit - Fundamental Measure of Control

### Definition

**Kybit:** The fundamental unit quantifying an agent's intentional influence on future outcomes, measured via Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence.

### Mathematical Foundation

The kybit measure quantifies total control when shifting from initial probability distribution P to final distribution Q:

**KL(Q||P) = Σ Q(x) log₂(Q(x)/P(x))**

Properties:
- Always non-negative (zero only if P = Q)
- Represents total thermodynamic work to rearrange probability distribution
- Robust to edge cases (zero-probability outcomes contribute zero by convention)

### Concrete Examples

**Forced Coin Flip:**
- Initial: P(heads) = 0.5, P(tails) = 0.5
- Final: Q(heads) = 1.0, Q(tails) = 0.0
- KL divergence = **1 kybit**
- Forcing uncertain outcome to certainty requires exactly 1 kybit

**Biased Die:**
- Initial: fair die, each face 1/6
- Final: face 1 = 0.5, face 2 = 0.25, faces 3-6 = 0.0625 each
- KL divergence ≈ **0.585 kybits**

### Physical Reality

Kybits represent real thermodynamic costs. Analogous to Landauer's principle:

**E_min = C · k · T · ln(2)**

where C = kybits of control, k = Boltzmann's constant, T = temperature.

**E_min = C · k · T · ln(2)**

## Part 5: The Law of Agency Decay (Second Law of Agency)

### Formal Statement

**"In a closed system without external energy input, the available capacity to exercise agency (kybits) inevitably decreases over time."**

Let:
- C_available(t) = kybits agent can exert at time t
- E_free(t) = available free energy

Then: **C_available(t) ∝ E_free(t)**

As free energy decreases → control capacity decays.

### Drift: The Silent Enemy

Entropy is always waiting. Without replenishment:
- Internal modeling degrades
- Action capabilities diminish
- Ability to bias futures shrinks
- Eventually: stop steering entirely, random drift reclaims dominance

### Implications

- **No perpetual agency** - requires continual intake of negentropy
- **No infinite choosing without cost**
- **No escape from thermodynamic consequences**

Sustained agency requires continuous energy from outside sources:
- Food
- Fuel
- Computation
- Knowledge

**Agency is a temporary island in a sea of entropy.**

### Real-World Manifestations

- Starving organisms lose agency (survival narrows to reflex)
- Depleted batteries in AI systems → shutdown
- Exhausted humans lose flexibility ("decision fatigue" is physically real)

At every scale, steering futures demands continuous energy investment.

## Part 6: The Law of Agency Limits (Third Law of Agency)

### Formal Statement

**"Perfect, frictionless control over future outcomes is physically impossible."**

As available free energy approaches zero:
**lim(E_free → 0) C_available → 0**

Consequence: **E_min > 0** for any C > 0

Perfect control (infinite kybits without thermodynamic cost) is impossible.

### Why Perfect Control Is Impossible

- Physical systems never entirely isolated from external disturbances (noise)
- Predictive models and internal simulations inherently imperfect and costly
- Prediction and action require irreversible physical processes that always generate entropy

Even under ideal conditions, agents must expend finite energy to steer outcomes.

**Infinite agency is an illusion. Frictionless will does not exist.**

### Real-World Constraints

- Supercomputers expend massive energy to simulate complex scenarios
- Human brains use substantial biological resources to plan, decide, act
- Organisms, ecosystems, civilizations inevitably face limits from energy reserves

Agency is meaningful **precisely because** it is limited and costly. Perfect victories impossible, but meaningful victories achievable - locally, temporarily, purposefully.

**Yes. Absolutely.**

Not because you select one exclusive future, but because **actions influence the measure of outcomes across branches.**

You don't eliminate futures; you guide how prominently "you" inhabit them.

### Measure: The Weight of Worlds

In MWI:
- Each branch carries **measure** - weight or "thickness"
- Determined by squared amplitude of quantum wavefunction component
- Higher measure = greater presence of observers experiencing that branch

**Agency influences how measure is distributed among preferred vs less preferred futures:**
- Intentional, model-driven actions → increase measure of certain outcomes
- Passive drift or reflexive actions → spread measure thinly and randomly

### How Agency Shapes Measure

When you:
1. Create internal predictive models
2. Anticipate possible outcomes
3. Act intentionally to favor certain outcomes

→ You actively engage environmental variables in ways that shape branching measure

**Your intentional efforts (kybits) don't remove possibilities; they redistribute measure among them.**

You guide the multiverse's unfolding by selectively amplifying certain branches.

### Why This Matters

In deterministic multiverse where every outcome exists:
- **Meaning is found in measure**
- **Agency is about influencing the flow toward particular futures**

You don't control what exists; **you control which outcomes flourish.**

Agency matters because intentional actions selectively amplify certain futures, shaping the landscape of your existence across the multiverse.

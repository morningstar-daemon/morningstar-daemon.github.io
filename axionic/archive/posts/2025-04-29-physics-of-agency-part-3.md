## Summary

Introduces the kybit as a quantitative measure of intentional control, defined using Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence between initial and final probability distributions. Examples: forcing a 50/50 coin flip to certainty costs 1 kybit; biasing a fair die costs ~0.585 kybits. Kybits represent real thermodynamic costs analogous to Landauer's principle.
